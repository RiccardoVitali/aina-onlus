AINA ONLUS

To say who we are is a very difficult task, we could say that AINA is the union of more complexity on several levels, of an encounter between different worlds and people all driven by the same desire, to help and support the other.

DISTANCE ADOPTION

AINA adheres to the “Guidelines for Distance Support of Children and Young People” issued by the Agency for Onlus. Distance Adoption is a form of solidarity that allows many, young and old, to receive education, food, clothes…

1992

The meeting in Africa in 1992 with the Little Missionary Sisters of Don Orione in the house of Igoji (Meru, Kenya) gave life - with the support of Sister Noemi - to a great dream.

1998

A.I.N.A. is part of the Coordination of Associations for Distance Scholastic Support “La Gabbianella” which brings together about 42 associations throughout Italy.

2000

In Argentina, A.I.N.A. has promoted the Comunità del Sole project that has allowed the construction of a neighborhood for 20 families in Barranqueras (Chaco).

2005

The project Bimbi del Meriggio is born to respond to the requests for help and collaboration received from the population and the local authorities of the District of Meru (Kenya).

2010

Inauguration of the family village “Bimbi del Meriggio” is celebrated the official opening of the centre. The event is attended by local institutions and the community linked to the village of Nchiru.

2014

Construction of the laundry room with two drying racks and construction of the artesian well necessary for the supply of water to the village.
